Parks/Hiking

  1. Jug Bay Wildlife Sanctuary: Jug Bay Wildlife Sanctuary features a set of trails, tours, and outdoor activites that can be enjoyed by the public. Jug bay is home to many different species of animals such as the Western Osprey, North American Beaver, and the Eastern Hognose Snake. Jug Bay Wetland Sanctuary also offers services to aid with camping, boating, and conservation
  2. Sugarloaf Mountain State Park: Sugarloaf Mountain is located in Frederick, MD and is a great place to go for a hike. It offers trails for people of all skill levels, allowing anyone to climb to the top of the mountain. The top offers great views, so be sure to bring your camera. I have only traveled to Sugarloaf once so far, but I enjoyed it and hope to hike it again soon.
  3. Cascade Fall at Pastaspco Valley: Patapsco Valley has multiple site locations, meaning the park is not located in one single location. Therefore, there are a number of paths and trails a person can explore. One of my favorite trails is located in Catonsivlle, MD and is home to a waterfall known as Cascade Falls.

Please note, some of these locations require an entrance fee.
